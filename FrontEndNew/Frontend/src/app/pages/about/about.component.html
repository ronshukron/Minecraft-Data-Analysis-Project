About the System:

Weâ€™ve developed a system for analyzing Minecraft gameplay using the MineRL database, which holds extensive data from various player missions, such as building a house or crafting a diamond pickaxe. This database is vast and complex, making it difficult to access. Our system simplifies access through a user-friendly website that offers customizable analysis of this high-volume data (around 10 TB), enabling users to draw insights about player behavior.

Our client, Roni Stern, and the SPL Lab at Ben-Gurion University, specialize in algorithm development in multi-agent planning, particularly focusing on the STRIP multi-agent model and solving multi-agent pathfinding problems. Minecraft provides an intriguing environment for this research due to its virtual world where players have significant freedom to explore and interact.

The project utilizes technologies like Angular, Node.js, Express etc. to facilitate this system. This system enhances research capabilities by making it easier to analyze and interpret player behaviors in a complex and dynamic environment.
